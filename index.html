The United States makes no sense. No, not its culture, or politics... The actual geographic boundaries of the states which comprise this great nation make no sense. There are 3 states along the west coast and 14 along the east coast. California’s borders contain a population of nearly 40 million while Wyoming’s hold less than 600,000 people. And most of Kansas City is in Missouri, which is just annoying.

Our state boundaries are more accidents of history than they are a reflection of common sense or the reality of right now. They were either drawn to address the politics of the time, or simply on a whim by men that never set foot in the land they carved up - plus it wasn't their land to carve up, but those are stories for another time.

If someone where ever to redraw state lines, the country would definitely need to be divided up in a more sensible way. One where states have more equitable populations and thus are represented fairly in government. One where metropolitan areas exist under a single state government so that services can be more easily managed. And one where geographically and culturally similar regions.
